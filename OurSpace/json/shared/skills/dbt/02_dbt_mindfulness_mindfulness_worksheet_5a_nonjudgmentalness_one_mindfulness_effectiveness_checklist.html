Shared life filter

Mindfulness is allowed to be tiny, sensory-safe, and done from bed or wheelchair. Noticing without judging counts even when the day is messy.

Tiny version
  • Notice one body signal.
  • Name one feeling or need.
  • Find one neutral thing in the room.
  • Return to the next safe action.
Access notes
  • Use eyes-open grounding if closing eyes feels unsafe.
  • Use quiet, low-scent, low-touch options for MCAS, trauma, and sensory overload.
  • No posture requirement; lying down or supported sitting is valid.
Us notes
  • William's needs and Jasper's capacity both count.
  • Ask before helping; ask before escalating; name the smallest next step.
  • Pause means pause, not abandonment. I am coming back. I care about us. I need a smaller next step.
  • What is needed, what capacity exists, what can be smaller, and what can wait?
